Orders arrive at a manufacturing company at random times (e.g., every 18-30 hours). Orders are processed at four consecutive machines M1-M4. Machines have variable processing times (that is, a machine may need, for instance, 1.5, 2 or 3 days to process an order). When an order is completed, this results in some revenue and manufacturing costs per order. Each work-in-progress item (waiting or partially processed but not completed) creates backlog costs per order.
